# **Developer Brief: SBTF1 Public Landing Page**

**Project:** STAG Blue Transformation Fund 1 (SBTF1)

**Developer Target:** Claude Code / React Architect

**Objective:** Build a high-performance, institutional-grade landing page that balances high-impact ocean imagery with clinical, PE-style operational data.

## **1\. Technical Stack & Architecture**

* **Framework:** React (Vite preferred) with Tailwind CSS.  
* **Component Library:** shadcn/ui (customized for "Ice & Deep" theme).  
* **Data Visualization:** Recharts or Framer Motion for SVG-based charts.  
* **Animation:** Framer Motion for scroll-linked visual devices.

## **2\. Design Tokens (The "Ice & Deep" Theme)**

* **Background:** Ice White (\#F8FAFC) \- The main layout must be cold and sterile.  
* **Typography:** \* Headings: Inter (Bold, Tracking: \-0.02em).  
  * Body: IBM Plex Sans (Technical touch).  
  * Numbers/Metrics: JetBrains Mono (High precision).  
* **Glassmorphism:** Use backdrop-blur(12px) and bg-white/70 for all cards overlaid on media.

## **3\. Visual Device: "Vertical Anchor & Vortex O"**

The page must feature a constant vertical line in the left-third gutter.

* **The Line:** A 1px solid line (\#001F3F with 15% opacity).  
* **The Vortex "O":** An SVG section marker that the line passes through at the beginning of each section.  
* **Interactive State:** The "O" should stand out in Teal (\#00A8A8) when the section is visible.

## **4\. Page Structure & Detailed Content (Source: Slides 4-10)**

### **Section 1: Hero (Source: Slide 4 \- Executive Summary)**

* **Media:** Cinematic full-bleed video of fish swimming in a powerful "vortex" pattern. This video serves as the organic literalization of the fund's visual identity (the "Vortex O").  
* **Headline:** "STAG Blue Transformation Fund 1: Investing in the Future of Global Food Security."  
* **Sub-headline:** "A private equity strategy in Portugal's blue economy, designed to build and scale category-leading businesses capturing value at multiple points of the seafood chain."  
* **Supporting Thesis Copy:**  
  * "Global food systems face a structural protein deficit as world demand for high-quality seafood continues to accelerate."  
  * "Europe remains structurally dependent on seafood imports due to a production and transformation base that is largely fragmented and undersized."  
  * "We implement a sector-focused strategy to create category leaders through a disciplined 'buy-and-build' approach, optimizing yields and waste valorization pathways."  
* **Core Metrics (Interactive Card):**  
  * **Target Size:** €125M  
  * **Target IRR:** 15% \- 20%  
  * **Term:** 10 Years  
  * **Alignment:** SDG 14

### **Section 2: Macro Context (Source: Slides 5-6 \- Global Food Systems & Seafood)**

* **Focus:** Demand Growth vs. Resource Constraints.  
* **Detailed Copy Points:**  
  * **Population & Production Gap:** "To keep up with population growth until 2050, world agricultural production will need to increase by **48.6%**. In fast-growing regions such as Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia, this requirement jumps to **112.4%**."  
  * **The Efficiency Problem:** "The global food system leaks value at scale. 30-40% of food produced is lost or goes uneaten, incurring economic costs of \~US$1 trillion per year."  
  * **Seafood Consumption Surge:** "Global per capita seafood consumption has seen unstoppable growth, rising from approximately **14 kg in 1990** to over **22 kg in 2020**."  
  * **The Growth Frontier:** "While wild captures are stagnant (predicted growth of only 10.4% until 2032), **aquaculture is the new frontier**, with a projected global growth of **17.4%**, reaching over 110 million tonnes."  
* **Data Visualization Strategy:**  
  1. **Production Gap Chart:** Comparative bar chart (using Recharts) showing the required percentage increase by region (World vs. Sub-Saharan Africa) based on Table 5.1.  
  2. **Consumption Trend:** Smooth area chart illustrating the rise from 14kg to 22kg per capita, contrasting with the decline in wild capture sustainability.  
  3. **The Great Divergence:** A grouped bar chart comparing "Captures" vs "Aquaculture" by region (2022 vs 2032), highlighting Asia's dominance and Europe's growth potential.

### **Section 3: The Funding Gap & SDG 14 (Source: Slide 7\)**

* **Headline:** "Harnessing the World's Purest Protein Source: The Funding Gap."  
* **Detailed Copy Points:**  
  * **The Opportunity:** "Achieving SDG 14 by 2030 requires \~€147 billion per year globally. Current annual investment is only \~€21 billion, leaving an annual deficit of €126 billion."  
  * **Ocean Impact:** "The ocean covers 70% of the Earth's surface, captures 90% of the heat generated by CO2 emissions, and absorbs 20% of all global CO2."  
* **UI Component:** A high-impact "SDG 14 Scorecard" showing the Required Financing vs. Current Reality.

### **Section 4: Marine Protein Efficiency (Source: Slides 8-9)**

* **Focus:** High-Yield, Low-Impact Food Systems.  
* **Interactive Comparison Data:**  
  * **Land Usage:** "Marine protein production requires up to 225 times less land and freshwater than terrestrial livestock. Farmed fish utilizes 90% less land and 98% less freshwater per kg of protein produced."  
  * **Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR):** \* Aquaculture: 1.2 \- 1.5  
    * Chicken: 1.7 \- 2.0  
    * Pork: 2.7 \- 5.0  
    * Beef: 6.0 \- 10.0  
  * **Carbon Footprint:** "Farmed fish generates 4-6 kg of CO2 equivalent per kg of protein, compared to 12 kg for pork and 27 kg for beef."  
* **UI Component:** A "Sustainability Calculator" or side-by-side comparative technical specifications.

### **Section 5: The European Deficit (Source: Slide 10\)**

* **Headline:** "The European Deficit: A €22B Structural Trade Imbalance."  
* **Detailed Copy Points:**  
  * **The Self-Sufficiency Crisis:** "EU self-sufficiency dropped from **46.1% in 2014** to just **37.5% in 2022**, reflecting a growing inability to meet internal demand."  
  * **Structural Dependency:** "Europe consumes **12% of global seafood production**, but produces only **3%**. This dependency results in a massive trade deficit of **€22 billion** in 2023."  
  * **Market Opportunity:** "This deficit is not a market failure, but a structural supply chain issue. For investors, this signal is clear: there is a unique opportunity for import substitution and margin capture through scalable aquaculture and modern infrastructure."  
* **Data Visualization Strategy:**  
  1. **Self-Sufficiency Line Chart:** Area chart showing the sharp decline from 46% to 37% (2013-2022).  
  2. **Trade Deficit Bar Chart:** Evolution of the trade deficit in billions of euros (2014-2023).  
  3. **Consumption Donut:** "Global Seafood Consumption" highlighting the EU (12%) vs. Rest of the world (88%).
